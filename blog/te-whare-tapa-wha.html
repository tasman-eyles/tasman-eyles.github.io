Introducing Te Whare Tapa Whā

What is Te Whare Tapa Whā?

The Te Whare Tapa Wha is a model developed to provide a maori perspective on health and wellbeing. The model was developed by Tā Mason Durie in 1984. There are 4 dimensions to the Te Whare Tapa Wha model:

  1. Taha Tinana (physical wellbeing)
  2. Taha Hinengaro (mental wellbeing)
  3. Taha Wairua (spiritual wellbeing)
  4. Taha Whānau (family wellbeing)

The Te Whare Tapa Wha model points out the importance of harmony between these 4 dimensions (walls of the whare) and how they relate to human wellbeing, physical and mental.
